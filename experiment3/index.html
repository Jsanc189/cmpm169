Experiment 3 - Generative Methods

Imitate

I started this art exploration with a piece by Yasai: https://openprocessing.org/sketch/494102. I first rewrote the code out and commented each line to make sure I understood what the artist was doing.

Integrate

From the previous step, I started changing the shape of what was being traced out by a noise function that was being utilized. I first started with a square. While it did make a slight change, it wasn't very drastic.

I then tried utilizing Triangles. This ended up making a bunch of various size triangles move on my screen. I decided to then try a Bezier Curve. I ended up with an unexpected reaction that reminded me of sunlight.

Innovate

After I saw the Bezier curve result, it reminded me so much of sunlight, I started implementing color pallets to try and replicate the moode of the sunlight. I started with warmer tones, but realize I wanted more contrast

After adding some contrasting colors and changing the background to a more complimentary color, I decided I wanted to have it look like it was streaming through the clouds.

I coded in an array of clouds that move slowly back and forth at the top of the screen. This took a lot of time and effort to figure out, as I initially wanted to use noise as well, but it did not achieve the effect I was after.

Reflection

This entire project was done by myself. The difficulties I had were initially getting the inspiration material to work. Utilizing Chat GPT, I was able to find a work around to at least mimic the original work and understand how things operated. From there, I just started messing around with the different shapes Noise could make. It was a total accident how I got something that looked like sunlight at dusk. It was so beautiful, that by the end, I was just making small changes to see how aesthetically pleasing I could get it (to myself). This project also intimidated me at first. After I got the initial simulation going, I was not sure where to go from there. Only my just asking myself, “I wonder what happens if I do this?” and went and did that thing, did the piece change and take on a different form. I ended up really enjoying the surprising things that happened when I changed something.
